Paris Tour : Most visited monuments in Paris

15 April 2020 france
  • Share
  • Tweet
  • Email

Bonjour! I know that you’ve heard a lot about Paris and how beautiful it is. You have always dreamed to watch the sunset while looking at the Eiffel Tower (better in couple ^^) ! Who have not been curious about walking at the champs élysées street and taking fancy photos.

Paris is the most visited cities in the world! It is full of amazing touristic monuments that attract millions of people all over the year. Also, It is a city of art, food, and romance…

Well, the are no enough words to describe the beauty of paris. Let’s take a quick tour in it.

  1. Eiffel Tower

    Eiffel Tower is the symbol of Paris. Designed by Gustave Eiffel, it was built in 1889 for the world’s fair. 300m of height, you can see the tower from all across the city.

    AdultYouth 12-14Children 4-11Children under 4
    16.608.304.10Free
    These prices are updated on 04/2020

  2. Champs Elysees Avenue

    A large street that strats from place de la Concorde untile the Arc de triomphe. It is a commercial city that hosts the worlds tops brands like Louis Vuitton, Lacoste, Abercrombie and Fitch,…

    You will never get bored in street because it has everything you want: You can have an afternoon walk along the street, buy a pain au chocolat, or do shopping, you can eat at the restaurant or taste the french wine…

  3. Arc de triomphe

    A unique view spot of the city can be seen from the top of Arc de triomphe. In addition to its location at the end of the Champs Elysees Avenue, Arc de triomphe is the center of place de l'étoile and 1.7 km close to the Eiffel tower. From the top you can see the 6 symetrics streets that who surrounds the arc de triomphe. I will let you imagine the view, or maybe you can look at the photo below!

    History : It was wanted by Napoleon in 1806 and inaugurated in 1836 by the french king Louis-Philippe.

    Price: 13 euros.

  4. Palace of Versailles

    The residential palace of the king from 1682. It represents the symbol of French monarchy until the revolution 1789. The palace is a series of architectural components spread on 63,154 m2. These are 2300 rooms and halls which 1000 of them are exposed for touristc visits.

    The parc of the Chateau de Versailles has a surface of 815 ha, where 93 ha are used for garden. This garden is piece of art that is maintainted until today. It has many fountains and a big lake where you can rent a boat and enjoy your day.

    Price : 18 euros minimum. ( multiple fares available).

  5. Louvre Museum

    The world largest museum and a historic monument. The louvre museum is located inside the palace of Louvre. This palace was the residential palace of the French kings before the Palace of Versailles.

    When Louis XIX moved to Versailles, he leaves the palaces as place full of of art sculpture and materpieces. In 1699, the first art salon has held in the Louvre palace. After the french revolution, the Louvre has been a museum for nation’s masterpieces.

    Price: 17 euros.

  6. Notre-Dame de Paris Cathedral

    It took 100 years to finish the Notre-Dame de Paris Cathedral in 1260. It is located in the Île de la Cité in the center of Paris near Saint Michel District. Indeed, this cathedral represents the symbol of the french gothic architecture.

  7. Saint Michel district

    A famous district for food and Wine. If you don’t know what to do in Paris, just go there and walk around. You will surely find a good restaurant to eat Fondue de fromage, or a french Wine de Bordeaux. could not find image

  8. Montmartre - Sacré Coeur de Paris

    A high hill in the 18th arrodissement of paris, it hosts the Sacré Coeur de Paris, one of the most visited monuments in Paris. It is the second most visited religious places in Paris after The Notre-Dame Cathedral

  9. Les invalides

    Famous for its golden dome, Les Invalides is nuilt as a retirement home for war veterans. Now, it contains a church and the tombs of some heroes of France , example : Napoleon.

  10. Petit Palais

    It is a museum of Fine Arts located near the Champs Elysees Avenue. Is is not crowded as the Louvre, but has some good art pieces.

    Price : Free (sometimes).

  11. Bridge Alexandre 3

    A bridge that connects the Invalides district to the Champs elysees district. It is the most famous bridge in Paris because of its four gilded fames.

  12. Jardin de Luxembourg

A large garden in the center of Paris city. It is a perfect place to read a book or relax.
